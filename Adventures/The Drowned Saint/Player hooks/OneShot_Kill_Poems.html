☠️ One-Shot's Kill Poems

To be spoken dramatically or mischievously whenever One-Shot uses Assassinate.

“A blink, a breath, a blade to bone—
You should’ve stayed inside your tomb.”
“You shambled, moaned, you reached to bite—
I whispered ‘nope’ and snuffed your light.”
“One step, two steps, you turned your head—
Too late. You’re missing it. (Your head.)”
“Rotten ribs and stinking soul—
I poked a hole. You’re not whole.”
“You were a priest, or so they said—
Now you’re just a pile of dread.”
“With silent toe and steady aim—
I end your cursed, forgotten name.”
“Undead or not, you have a spine—
I borrowed it. It’s now mine.”
“You hissed, you missed, I kissed your fate—
My dagger’s faster than your hate.”
“Rest in peace? You didn’t earn it—
I helped. You’re welcome. Don’t return it.”
“I counted steps, I held my breath—
I whispered poetry... and death.”
