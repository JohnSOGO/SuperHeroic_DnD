Aloquin’s Role in “The Drowned Saint”

Plot Hooks Specific to Aloquin

  • The Counted Dead: Aloquin has freed 311 of the 1,021 required souls. The Soul-Drinker Idol presents a major opportunity—dozens, perhaps hundreds of souls are trapped within.
  • Village Echoes: The haunted moans within the Idol resemble those Aloquin heard in the ruins of Vensborough, drawing painful memories—and hope of redemption.
  • Deathwarden’s Voice: The patron may speak directly to Aloquin near the Idol, compelling or warning him depending on his actions.

The Pact with the Deathwarden

Aloquin is bound to a sacred pact: release 1,021 defiled souls to free his family and find peace in death. Until then, he rises after every death. His very existence is a walking reminder of his purpose—and failure.

Consequence: If Aloquin tries to absorb the Idol for power or delay judgment, the Deathwarden may curse him further—or sever the pact entirely.

How the Idol Tempts Aloquin

  • Power: The Idol could give him a shortcut—release all souls at once by absorbing them through a devastating act of sacrifice.
  • Despair: Voices from the Idol whisper of futility, urging Aloquin to take the burden alone and use the souls to burn away evil preemptively.
  • Personal Echoes: One voice in the Idol may sound like his younger brother Casrin—possibly an illusion or a soul truly trapped.

Optional Souls for Release

The Idol contains over 100 trapped spirits, including:

  • The original Order of the Kindled Mercy (Elira’s companions)
  • Defiled townsfolk of Vellbridge
  • Corrupted clergy who repented too late

Choices During the Adventure

  • Seal the Idol: Elira is freed, and Aloquin adds all 100+ souls to his tally. He gains favor with the Deathwarden.
  • Absorb the Idol: Aloquin consumes the Idol, gaining a massive temporary power boost. He adds the souls to his count, but suffers torment, permanent scars, and alienation from his patron.
  • Destroy the Idol: All souls are released indiscriminately. Aloquin adds some souls (roll randomly) but risks corruption and divine backlash.

DM Note:

This adventure is Aloquin’s crossroads. The Idol is a crucible—not just for Elira’s fate, but for Aloquin’s legacy. Use divine visions, voices in dreams, or flashes of past trauma to draw him deeper.
