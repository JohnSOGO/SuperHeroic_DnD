📜 Whispering Vestry – The Bishop’s Final Confession

🎭 DM Script: What the Players Experience

The vestry is unnervingly silent. Even your footsteps sound distant, swallowed by stone and shadow. On a simple wooden bench, beneath a cracked iron wall sconce, rests a leather-bound journal wrapped in a strip of priestly sash. As you open the book, the air seems to tighten — as if the room is listening.

📖 Items Found in the Room

  • The Bishop’s personal journal, scorched along one edge
  • A broken signet ring engraved with the name Ennius Cauldran
  • Wax-sealed envelope, never sent, addressed to “Mother Solara”
  • A Kindled Mercy rosary twisted into a loop — several beads shattered
  • An empty vial labeled “Mercy’s Silence”

📝 Journal Entry – Final Confession of Bishop Ennius Cauldran

I knew her light would spread too far. Elira saw healing in pain, mercy in blood. I saw only threat — that she would eclipse me, and call down judgement on the rest of us.

I lied. I forged the vision. I whispered to the Arch-Clerics that she had grown proud, that she would betray her vows.

But it was I who betrayed the vow. I feared the flame she carried. I feared that her mercy would reveal the rot in our bones.

I gave the order. I watched them seal her away beneath stone and prayer, and I said nothing.

Now the Idol speaks to me. It knows what I did. It feeds on the truth I buried.

If there is forgiveness, it must come from her lips. But she is gone, and I — I have no right to rise.

May this room, this silence, carry my confession to whatever gods still listen.
