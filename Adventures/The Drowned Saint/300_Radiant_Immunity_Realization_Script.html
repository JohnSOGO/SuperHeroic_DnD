☀️ Radiant Immunity – DM Script & Lore Justification

📜 DM Read-Aloud Script

Your spell flares with holy brilliance, a blast of radiant light searing through the sanctified air —

— and then... nothing.

The creature stands unbothered, bathed in the glow, its form unmoving. The light crackles and disperses like it’s been swallowed.

“That light no longer touches us,” a voice whispers, “we remember its warmth... but we serve deeper truths now.”

🔍 Lore Reason: Why Are These Creatures Immune to Radiant?

Based on The Drowned Saint narrative and character files (SuperHeroic DnD.pdf, Elira’s lore, statblocks):

  • These undead were once part of the Kindled Mercy – Elira’s holy order dedicated to radiant healing and mercy.
  • After Elira’s betrayal and burial, many clergy renounced the light or were twisted by grief and guilt.
  • Exposure to the Soul-Drinker Idol warped them into undead forms that reject the divine light.
  • They are no longer harmed by the radiance they once wielded — their souls have become numb to grace.
  • Symbolically, this makes them immune to radiant damage — light is now meaningless to them.

🧠 Tips for Players

  • If the player asks why, Chris or Aloquin may recognize that “they were once of the light… and now they’re beyond it.”
  • One-Shot might notice that even shadows flicker off them unnaturally — they are unanchored.
  • Encourage alternate tactics: necrotic, fire, weapons, physical traps.

🎭 Narrative Meaning

This twist reinforces the story of spiritual corruption — not just physical decay. The undead are not merely monsters. They are burned-out saints, numb to the very light that once guided them.
