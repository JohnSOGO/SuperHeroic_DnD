🔔 Bell Tower Scenario: The Rope Trap

The bell tower of the drowned cathedral rises above the surrounding ruins — accessible by a winding, decayed spiral stair. At its peak hangs a cracked bell, covered in oxidized inscriptions and ritual markings.

🪢 The Trap

  • A frayed rope dangles from the center of the tower, tied to a series of pressure anchors and counterweights hidden in the frame.
  • The entire assembly is rigged: pulling the rope triggers a chain collapse of upper support beams — dropping 500 lbs of sanctified stone rubble downward.
  • Any creature directly below must make a DC 15 Dexterity saving throw or take 3d10 bludgeoning damage and be knocked prone.
  • The trap can be noticed with DC 14 Investigation or Passive Perception ≥ 15.

🎯 One-Shot’s Moment

One-Shot recognizes a faint symbol etched near the base of the rope — a Thieves’ Cant glyph for “DEATH FROM ABOVE.”

  • With a DC 13 Sleight of Hand or Thieves' Tools check, she can disarm the trap or trace the rope to a safe pull point.
  • If she succeeds, she finds a hidden release mechanism that opens a cracked panel in the tower’s outer wall — leading to a concealed relic chamber.
  • Inside is a scroll of silence and a copper prayer bowl

🧠 DM Tips

  • This trap rewards caution, rogue skills, and creative thinking.
  • If triggered, the falling rubble could **block the stairwell** — forcing players to find a new route or retreat.
  • The relic room can contain tools useful in the final ritual or a journal from one of Elira’s forgotten sisters.
“They thought the bell warned the faithful — but it only summoned death.”
